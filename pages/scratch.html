Why SCRATCH?

Scratch is a graphical programming language, where puzzle pieces acting as lines of code are draged, droped, and put together to create fun interctive programs.

My reasons for choosing SCRATCH as my first programming lanaguage are two fold and can best be summed up by Harvard Professor David J. Malan. In his research entitled "Scratch for Budding Computer Scientists", Professor Malan along with Henry H. Leitner discovered that SCRATCH allowed first time programmers to "not only master programmatic constructs before syntax but also to focus on problems of logic before syntax."

For me SCRATCH was the perfect gateway into langauges like Python. Not only did SCRATCH famailiarize me, someone who was inexpereinced, with the fundamentals of programming (ie variales, loops, conditions, boolean expressions, functions, events...), it excited me and gave me the confidence to jump straight into Python after learning how to use the SCRATCH environment and working on a project (see below)
